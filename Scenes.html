Old Alleys & Communities

Wander through Chengdu’s quiet, tree-lined alleys where locals live, work, and chat. Stroll past cozy street-side cafes, golden ginkgo-lined streets in autumn, and colorful lantern-lit lanes that feel like stepping into a story. This is the real Chengdu—slow, warm, and full of small, beautiful moments you won’t find in tourist guides.

Our local guides will introduce you to residents, share stories about the alley’s history, and take you to hidden snack spots loved by locals. You’ll taste authentic Sichuan street food, watch elders play mahjong under old trees, and experience the laid-back lifestyle that makes Chengdu so special.

Local Teahouses & Cafes

Slow down and savor Chengdu’s iconic tea culture in century-old teahouses, where you can sip Sichuan tea while listening to folk music performances. We also take you to cozy, plant-filled cafes and tranquil courtyard spaces—perfect for unwinding, working, or simply enjoying the city’s laid-back vibe.

This is where Chengdu’s soul truly breathes. Our guides will share the history of Sichuan tea, teach you how to brew it properly, and recommend the best local snacks to pair with your tea. You’ll also have the chance to learn diancha, a traditional whipped tea technique that dates back to the Song Dynasty.

Local Markets & Events

Dive into Chengdu’s vibrant local life at bustling markets and community events. Wander through night markets filled with spicy Sichuan street food, browse creative craft fairs where local artisans sell handmade jewelry and textiles, and join cultural festivals that celebrate Sichuan’s traditions.

Our guides will take you to markets that are off the tourist radar, where you can bargain for unique souvenirs, taste fresh local fruits, and interact with vendors who have been selling their goods for generations. You’ll also have the chance to join local events like tea ceremonies, calligraphy workshops, and traditional dance performances.

Chengdu City Parks

Chengdu is a city of parks, where locals gather to relax, exercise, and socialize. Explore serene green spaces filled with blooming flowers, ancient trees, and tranquil lakes. Watch locals practice tai chi, fly kites, or play traditional musical instruments.

Our guides will take you to the most beautiful and authentic parks, away from the tourist crowds. You’ll have the chance to join in local activities, like calligraphy writing on the ground with water, or simply sit and enjoy a picnic while watching the world go by. These parks are a window into the daily life and relaxed spirit of Chengdu.

Suburban Hiking & Outdoor

Escape the city and explore Chengdu’s stunning natural landscapes. Hike through lush bamboo forests, discover hidden waterfalls, and enjoy panoramic views of mountains and valleys. Our hiking trails are carefully selected for their beauty and tranquility, away from the crowds.

Our guides are experienced hikers who know the best routes and can tailor the difficulty to your group’s fitness level. You’ll have the chance to breathe fresh air, listen to birdsong, and experience the peace of the Sichuan countryside. This is the perfect way to relax and connect with nature while still being close to the city.

Sichuan Handcraft & Culture

Dive deep into Sichuan’s rich cultural heritage with hands-on workshops led by local masters. Learn traditional crafts like bamboo weaving, Sichuan embroidery, clay sculpting, and luodian inlay. These workshops are a unique opportunity to connect with the region’s history and creativity.

Our guides will take you to authentic studios where artisans have been practicing their craft for generations. You’ll not only learn a new skill but also gain a deeper appreciation for the time, effort, and artistry that goes into each piece. This is more than just a workshop—it’s a chance to create a personal souvenir that tells a story of your time in Chengdu.

Authentic Chengdu Food

No trip to Chengdu is complete without exploring its world-famous food scene. From fiery Sichuan hot pot to delicate street snacks, we’ll take you to the most authentic local eateries—away from tourist traps, where the real flavor of Chengdu lives.

Our guides know the best hidden spots: try mapo tofu at a family-run restaurant, savor spicy noodles at a street stall, or taste sweet glutinous rice balls at a century-old shop. Every bite tells a story of Chengdu’s culinary heritage.
